Helping Kids Walk to School


Encouraging Kids to Walk to School by Following a Robot


BACK TO HOME







Cars have taken over our society in many ways giving us a new way of transporting ourselves to different destinations. Because of the nasty habit that cars are polluting the air, it has come to the community's concern that we need to start finding new eco-friendly ways of getting from one location to another.


Walking, cycling and scooting has been a good alternative to these machines, however, not many people are willing to do this. So we have decided to work with schools along with the Whittlesea Council to encourage students to walk their way to school by initialising a robot that follows lines. These lines make their way to school. To be brief, the students can follow the robot to school.


The robot will use several sensors to detect the environment around it and detect any obstacles and will respond accordingly. The robot will also have an infared sensor underneath so that it can detect if it is on the white line or not and stay on the white lines. It will also be going at a slow pace so that the 'slow' walkers can still keep up.


The robot will also be able to read QR codes which will give the robot certain instructions and do those instructions.


Another great feature of the robot is that it will have a recharging station at school which is vandalism-proof. The recharging station will use eco-friendly ways to charge the robot so that it doesn't cause extra pollution on top of the already-accumulating amount in pollution.


It will also have GPS tracking which will allow parents know where their children currently are. This will be in an application which also check a variety of other vital things.
