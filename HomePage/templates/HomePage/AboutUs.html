THE MILL PARK LIBRARY MAKER'S CLUB, IN ASSOCIATION WITH THE WHITTLESEA COUNCIL, YPRL AND MELBOURNE WATER ARE CONDUCTING SEVERAL SYSTEMS TO HELP THE COMMUNITY.

THE WHITTLESEA COUNCIL IS WORKING WITH THE MILL PARK LIBRARY MAKER'S CLUB ORGANISATION TO SOLVE MANY PROBLEMS AROUND THE COMMUNITY. WE ALSO LIKE TO EDUCATE AND ENCOURAGE PEOPLE SPECIFICALLY STUDENTS, ABOUT TECHNOLOGY AND HOW THE SYSTEM WORKS. THE MAIN REASON IS SO THAT PEOPLE CAN MAKE A DIFFERENCE AND MAKE GOOD CHOICES IN THE COMMUNITY.

THE ORGANISATION IS ALSO WORKING WITH DEAKIN UNIVERSITY ON THE WATER QUALITY MONITORING PROJECT AND SOME SCHOOLS HAVE ALSO CONTRIBUTED IN BOTH THE RAINWATER USAGE MONITORING PROJECT AND THE 'HELPING KIDS TO WALK TO SCHOOL INITIATIVE'.

The organisation is located in the Mill Park Library and the team regularly meets on the Wednesday every week.
